Design direction v3 — same colors, new world: canvas, not console

The night studio

A dark idea canvas — the dot grid of a thinking tool, not the gutter of a code editor. Work floats on the canvas in soft washes of light; nothing is boxed, nothing is terminal. The five hues keep their jobs: gold is you, cyan is the machine, violet is the coach, green and red only ever report verdicts.

A role scoped to the reader

Not "helpful assistant" — the role names who consumes the output, which steadies tone and detail across daily runs.

Why for this model: XML sections are claude-sonnet-5's preferred structure, per its card.

Quotes before claims

Requiring a supporting quote per fact sharply cuts invented details — your stated failure mode.

A contract, not a plea

The CRM script is the consumer, so the format is a schema — and it doubles as this prompt's smoke check.

Long content goes last

Instructions first, transcript at the end — the stable prefix stays cacheable on recurring runs.

Why for this model: its card flags position sensitivity past 10k tokens.

The rules

What the canvas refuses

No chrome

No borders, gutters, sigils, or status bars. Work floats on the canvas in washes of light; hierarchy comes from scale and hue.

Hue is meaning

Gold is you, cyan is the machine, violet is the coach. Green and red only ever report verdicts from real runs.

Stale things fade

Health is luminance. Untested work dims until re-verified — attention goes where the light is.

Your words lead

The ask is the headline, set larger than anything the machine says back. The tool's voice never upstages yours.

Night Studio is dark-only by commitment — one visual world, executed fully. Monospace survives in exactly one place: inside payloads and measurements, where exactness is the message. Everything else speaks in rounded display type and soft light.
