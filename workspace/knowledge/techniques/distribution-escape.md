name: Distribution escape
lever: clarity
helps: creative and design generation (frontends, writing, naming) where the default output is generic "AI slop"
hurts: tasks where the conventional answer is the right answer
---
Models converge to the center of their training distribution — the generic
dashboard, the purple-gradient hero, the same five fonts. Escaping takes explicit,
named counter-pressure: tell the model it converges and what to avoid BY NAME
("avoid Inter, Roboto, purple gradients on white, centered hero layouts"), then
give it a direction to commit to ("draw from IDE themes; dominant color with sharp
accents beats timid palettes").

**Mechanism**: naming the clichés moves them from "high-probability default" to
"explicitly excluded," and a committed direction replaces the vacuum that
convergence fills. Vary your own examples too — the model imitates their spread.

**Refinements from the official cookbook**: steer specific design dimensions
individually (typography, color, motion, backgrounds) rather than one global vibe
line; and the productized form is a skill that forces choosing a named aesthetic
direction *before* generation — the reusable version of "give it a direction to
commit to."
